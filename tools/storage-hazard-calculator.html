What it does

Storage sprinkler protection is one of the trickiest calls in fire sprinkler design — the right scheme depends on commodity classification, storage method, storage and ceiling height, and aisle width, and the figures live across many NFPA 13 sections. This tool walks the criteria for you and lays out every applicable option:

  • CMDA (control mode density/area) ceiling-only and with in-rack sprinklers
  • CMSA (control mode specific application)
  • ESFR (early suppression fast response)
  • In-rack sprinkler arrangements

For each option you get the ceiling density and design area, in-rack demand, hose allowance, total water demand, and the figure/section it came from — so you can compare schemes and justify the one you pick.

How to use it

  1. Choose your NFPA 13 edition and commodity (e.g., cartoned unexpanded Group A plastic).
  2. Set the storage arrangement, storage height, ceiling height, and aisle width.
  3. Review the recommended criteria and filter by protection type.
  4. Copy the design text straight into your calc package or narrative.
