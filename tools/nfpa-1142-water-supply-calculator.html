What it does

NFPA 1142 sizes the water supply for rural and suburban firefighting where a reliable hydrant or waterworks isn't available. The tool works the standard's method for you:

  • Total structure volume from length, width, and height
  • Construction classification factor
  • Occupancy hazard classification number

FAQ

What is NFPA 1142?
NFPA 1142 is the Standard on Water Supplies for Suburban and Rural Firefighting. It provides a method for determining the minimum water supply needed for manual firefighting where hydrants or a public waterworks are not available.
When do you use NFPA 1142 instead of NFPA 13?
NFPA 1142 addresses the water supply available for firefighting a structure that is not protected by an automatic sprinkler system with an adequate supply. It is not a substitute for an NFPA 13 hydraulic design; it answers a different question.
Is this an official calculation?
It applies the NFPA 1142 method to your inputs to give a fast, documented estimate. Always verify against the current edition of the standard and the authority having jurisdiction.
